When to use it

Proposition testing is a way to quickly identify if ideas for new or updated websites or apps will meet user / customer needs (e.g. like the Dulux Visualizer) before starting to build them. Performing this sort of research is useful to advise our clients on whether or not ideas for new websites, apps, features or content are worth investing time and money in.

It is best to test propositions as early on in the design process as possible (you may test multiple in a single research study). This is done by creating sketches, wireframes or prototypes to quickly communicate a client’s / our idea(s), then testing it with users / customers.

The type of research and time it takes to perform in order to provide the evidence enabling your client to make a decision will vary. In the user research is vital to test in an environment as close to users’ real life circumstances as possible.

Proposition testing is best performed when our client’s (or ourselves) are unsure if ideas for new features, functionality and content will deliver on both business and user needs.
